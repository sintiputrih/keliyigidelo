Urgent Appeal: Help Locate Missing Henry Buster in Lone Tree, Colorado

 Posted on February 17, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  435 words  |  Martina Birk

A community in Lone Tree, Colorado, is in a state of distress following the sudden disappearance of 17-year-old Henry Buster. The details surrounding his case are concerning, and local law enforcement, along with Henry’s family, are urging anyone with information to come forward.

Who is Henry Buster?

Henry Buster is a 17-year-old resident of Lone Tree, Colorado. Described as kind and wonderful by those who know him, Henry has recently endured the loss of his father. As a result, he may be experiencing a mental health crisis, adding an extra layer of urgency to his case. He is about 6’ feet tall with brown curly hair.

What Happened To Henry Buster?

Henry was last seen in the early morning hours around 4:00 am on Sunset Hill Dr., in the Heritage Hills neighborhood. According to reports, he struck a gate and then fled on foot. Notably, he left without his phone or wallet, which potentially limits his means for communication and resources. He was last seen wearing a flannel shirt, sweat pants, and a white hat.

Is Henry Buster Found or Not?

As of now, the search for Henry Buster continues. The lack of significant leads or updates has left the community and his family in a state of anxious uncertainty. His mother, Carin Buster, is particularly worried and has been actively seeking any information that could lead to her son’s safe return.

The Lone Tree Police Department is taking the lead in the investigation and search efforts. They encourage anyone who may have seen Henry or have any information about his whereabouts to come forward.

If you or someone you know has any information about Henry Buster, please contact the Lone Tree police or Carin Buster directly at 303-810-5961. Your help could make a significant difference in bringing Henry home safely.

Why was Michael Jordan drafted 3rd?

 Posted on March 24, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  678 words  |  Martina Birk

When we talk about the 1984 NBA Draft, one of the most intriguing questions that often comes up is, "Why was Michael Jordan drafted 3rd?" Michael Jordan, widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time, was indeed selected as the third overall pick by the Chicago Bulls in that historic draft. 

The 1984 NBA Draft

The 1984 NBA Draft is widely considered one of the most talent-laden drafts in the history of the league. Notably, it produced several future Hall of Famers, including Hakeem Olajuwon, Michael Jordan, Charles Barkley, and John Stockton.

The Portland Trail Blazers held the number two pick, and the Houston Rockets had the first overall selection. The Chicago Bulls, who eventually landed Michael Jordan, held the third pick.

Factors Contributing to the Draft Position

Several factors played a significant role in Michael Jordan being drafted as the third overall pick in the 1984 NBA Draft. These factors include:

Draft Strategy and Team Needs

  • Team Priorities: The Houston Rockets, holding the first pick, were in need of a center, which led them to select Hakeem Olajuwon, a dominant force in the paint. The Portland Trail Blazers, with the second pick, were set at the shooting guard position with Jim Paxson, thus influencing their decision to pass on drafting Michael Jordan.

  • Chicago Bulls' Evaluation: The Chicago Bulls, with the third pick, were seeking a player who could make an immediate impact and help turn around the struggling franchise. At the time, they had a talented young shooting guard in Orlando Woolridge, which might have led them to focus on other positions of need.

Scouting and Perceived Potential

  • Scouting Reports: While Michael Jordan was highly regarded coming out of the University of North Carolina, there were questions about his ability to lead a team to a championship at the professional level. Also, some scouts and analysts believed that big men had a more significant impact on the game than guards, which might have influenced the draft decisions.

  • Potential versus Certainty: The concept of drafting for potential versus drafting for proven ability is a perennial debate in sports. It's possible that the teams drafting ahead of the Bulls were more inclined to select players they believed could fill specific roles immediately, whereas Jordan's potential to become a transformative player might have been viewed as a riskier proposition.

Historical Context and Perceived Talent

  • Guard-Heavy League: In the 1980s, the NBA was dominated by teams with exceptional big men, and there might have been a prevailing sentiment that a dominant center could have a more significant impact on a team's success than a shooting guard, thereby influencing the draft choices.

  • Perceived Talent Pool: The 1984 draft class boasted an extraordinary level of talent, making the decision-making process even more challenging for the teams. The presence of other top-tier prospects like Olajuwon, Sam Bowie, and Charles Barkley might have led to a distribution of talent across various positions rather than a heavy focus on guards.

Impact and Legacy

Despite being selected third overall, Michael Jordan went on to have a legendary career that solidified his status as one of the greatest basketball players in history.

His impact on the Chicago Bulls and the NBA as a whole is immeasurable, and his six championships, five MVP awards, and countless accolades speak to the remarkable career that followed his draft selection.

 

To sum things up, the factors that led to Michael Jordan being drafted as the third overall pick in the 1984 NBA Draft were multifaceted and influenced by team priorities, scouting evaluations, historical context, and perceived talent distribution.

While it may have been surprising at the time, Jordan's subsequent career not only justified his eventual status as the greatest player of his era but also continues to shape the way we perceive talent and potential in sports.

The 1984 NBA Draft will forever be a pivotal moment in basketball history, and the decision to select Michael Jordan as the third overall pick remains a topic of fascination and debate among fans and analysts to this day.

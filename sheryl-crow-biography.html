Who is Sheryl Crow?

Sheryl Crow is a popular American musician, singer, songwriter, and actress.

She has recorded her eight studio albums and has given voice to several film soundtracks.

She has now marketed over 50 million albums.

Crow has collected nine Grammy Awards (out of 32 nominations) from the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences.

Sheryl Crow: Birth Facts, Family, and Childhood

Sheryl was born on February 11, 1962, in Kennett, Missouri. Her mother’s name is Bernice Crow she was a piano teacher.

Her father’s name is Wendell Wyatt Crow he is a lawyer and trumpet player.

She has two older sisters and a younger brother in her family named Kathy and Karen and Steven.

Education History

She studied at Kennett High School in 1980 and completed her schooling.

Then she completed her graduated from The University of Missouri at Columbia in 1984.

During college, she spent her weekends performing with a local band, “Cashmere”.

After completing her graduation, Crow served as a music teacher.

Sheryl Crow: Early, Profession life and Career

Sheryl Crow began playing piano at age 6. After college, Crow worked. And she joined an elementary school in St. Louis teaching there as a music teacher in 1986.

After that, she also began to record different jingles for advertising clients, and also worked as a backup singer.

In 1987-’88 she got the chance to perform with Michael Jackson and sang on “Bad” on a world tour.

She later sang backup for Sting, Rod Stewart, and Don Henley. In 1991, Crow was called A&M Records for the recording of an album.

She performs there but it couldn’t reach the expectation.

So she started to play with the band name The Tuesday Music Club including singers like Bill Bottrell`, David Baerwald, and David Ricketts.

There was also Crow’s then-boyfriend Kevin Gilbert in the band.

In 1993, the group worked together and released the album named Tuesday Night Music Club.

The album was very popular including the hit, “All I Wanna Do.” She has also once appeared on the Late Show with David Letterman.

She claimed on the show that the song, “Leaving Las Vegas” was autobiographical.

After this incident, the band decided that Sheryl should be on her own and started to bring out her singles.

Her singles were also nice. She also performed an MTV Unplugged session in 1995. Crow then released the album, Sheryl Crow, in 1996.

After that, she was diagnosed with breast cancer and was involved in various health problems.

Though she was well enough after that, she went on her Stop Global Warming College Tour in April 2007.

She there started to perform different concerts. As her dream was to be a successful mother, Crow adopted her son Wyatt that year.

Again in 2008, she was back, Crow was back with her album Detours.

In Aug 2008, we saw her involved in an environmental-themed event related to the Democratic National Convention.

In 2010, Crow released her seventh studio album, 100 Miles From Memphis and it was also loved by all of her fans.

She again released the Feels Like Home album after three years. her first country music project, which successfully debuted in the top 10 on the Billboard 200.

Lifetime Achievements and Awards

Crow has received the award including the Grammy Award for Best New Artist, the Record of the Year, and also the Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance for the song All I Wanna Do in 1995.

Again in 1999 and 1997, she received  Grammy Awards for Best Rock Album for The Globe Sessions.

She receives Grammy Awards for Best Female Rock Vocal Performance also in 2003, 2001, 2000, … for her songs like ‘Steve McQueen, There Goes the Neighborhood, Sweet Child O’ Mine’.

And they awarded her the Academy of Country Music Award for Vocal Event of the Year in 2007 for ‘Building Bridges’.

Soon, again she was awarded American Music Award for Favorite Pop/Rock Female Artist in 2004, and 2003.

Likewise, Brit Awards for International Female Solo Artists in 1997. She received American Music Award for Favorite Adult Contemporary Artist in 2004, and the People’s Choice Award for Favorite Remake in 2005 for the song ‘The First Cut Is the Deepest’.

She also received the Glamour Woman of the Year Award in 2000.

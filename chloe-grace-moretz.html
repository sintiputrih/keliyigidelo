Career

Chloe Grace Moretz started her career as a television actress in the industry. She made her debut appearance in the popular television series titled The Guardian as a child artist. After that, she made her appearance in the popular film titled Heart of the Beholder in the year 2004.

In the year 2005, she made her appearance in the movie The Amityville Horror after which she gained appreciation and recognition in the industry. After this movie, she made many guest appearances in popular movies and television shows like Dirty Sexy Money, Big Momma’s House 2, Desperate Housewives, and others.

After that in the year 2010, she made her appearance in the movie Kick-Ass which proved to be a breakthrough in her career. Since then she has worked in movies like Let Me In, Texas Killing Fields, The Invention of Hugo Cabret, Hick, Dark Shadows, and others.

In the year 2013, she has been featured in the sequel of the movie Kick-Ass 2, and in the same year, she has also appeared in Movie 43. Along with these popular roles she has also given her voice for video games like Kick-Ass The Game.

In the year 2016, she became a member of the Narrative Short Film Competition jury for the Tribeca Film Festival and in the same year, she made her appearances in the movies titled Neighbors 2 Sorority Rising and Brain on Fire. The next year she made her appearances in the movies titled November Criminals and I Love You, Daddy.

In the year 2018, she made her appearance in the movie titled The Miseducation of Cameron Post along with Sasha Lane and Forrest Goodluck. In the same year, she has also appeared in the movie Suspiria which didn’t perform well at the box office. In the year 2019, she has also worked in movies like Greta, The Addams Family, and others.

Recently, in the year 2021, she has been featured in Hollywood films like Tom & Jerry, Shadow in the Cloud, and signed the film titled Mother/Android. Along with her professional acting career, she has also appeared in several photo shoots, editorials, features, and covers for magazines like Flaunt, Vogue, Teen Vogue, Elle Love, Interview, and many other popular magazines.

She has completed several modeling assignments and endorses several popular brands like Aeropostale and others. She has also been awarded the title of Max Mara Face of the Future at Paris Fashion Week in the year 2013.

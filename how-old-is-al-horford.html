How old is Al Horford?

Al Horford, known by the nickname "Big Al," is a prominent professional basketball player in the NBA. Born on June 3, 1986, in the Dominican Republic, Horford has had an illustrious career spanning over a decade. This article delves into Al Horford's age and his remarkable journey in the world of basketball.

Early Life and Education

Horford's Birth and Family Background

Alfred Joel Horford Reynoso was born on June 3, 1986, in the Dominican Republic. His family including his father and uncle, had a basketball background. They moved to Michigan when Horford was young, where he attended high school.

High School Years and AAU Basketball

Horford enrolled at Grand Ledge High School in Michigan. He distinguished himself by setting several records at the school During this time, he also played AAU basketball for the Michigan Mustangs.

College Career and Championships

University of Florida and NCAA Championships

Horford played college basketball for the University of Florida. He was a crucial member of the Florida Gators team. In 2006, Horford helped the Gators win the NCAA championship. He continued his success by contributing to the team's back-to-back championships in 2006 and 2007.

NBA Career

Draft and Atlanta Hawks

In the 2007 NBA Draft, Horford was selected with the third overall pick by the Atlanta Hawks. He spent nine seasons with the Hawks, establishing himself as a versatile and talented player.

Boston Celtics and All-Star Appearances

Horford joined the Boston Celtics in 2016. He became a five-time NBA All-Star during his tenure with the Celtics.

Philadelphia 76ers and Oklahoma City Thunder

Horford played for the Philadelphia 76ers and the Oklahoma City Thunder after leaving the Celtics. However, he returned to the Celtics in 2021.

Philanthropy and Charitable Contributions

Support for Various Causes

Throughout his career, Horford has been actively involved in charitable endeavors. He supported relief efforts in Japan and the Pacific region after a devastating earthquake and tsunami. Horford has collaborated with organizations such as Basketball Without Borders and the Make-A-Wish Foundation.

Coronavirus Relief Efforts

In 2020, Horford donated $500,000 towards coronavirus relief efforts. His contributions were directed towards the Dominican Republic and the regions of the United States where he has played.

Current Performance and Age

Recent Performance

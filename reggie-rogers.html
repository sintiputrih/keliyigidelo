Reggie Rogers is one of the most popular and richest American Football Player who was born on January 21, 1964 in Sacramento, California, United States. Reginald O’Keith Rogers, born January 21, 1964 and died October 24, 2013, was an American professional football defensive tackle. He played four seasons with the National Football League for both the Detroit Lions (1987-1988), Buffalo Bills(91) and Tampa Bay Buccaneers (92).

Rogers was involved with a hit-and run collision in Tukwila on November 26, 2008. This led to his arrest and a DUI charge. This was Rogers’ fifth DUI arrest in Washington since his time at UW.

At age 49, Rogers was found dead at his home in Seattle in 2013. An autopsy revealed that he died of combined cocaine and alcohol intoxication.

Rogers was released from prison in 1992. He had briefly played for Tampa Bay and Buffalo during his sentence. Rogers made his way to Canada and played for the Hamilton Tiger-Cats (1993-94), and the Shreveport Pirates (1996). He played 33 games for the CFL, and accumulated 18 tackles and 91 sacks in three down football.

Early Life & Biography

Ezra Klein was born in the year 1984 on 9 May in Irvine, California, the United States of America to a Jewish family. He is a middle child of Abel Klein, his father, a mathematics professor at the University of California, Irvine, originally from Brazil, while his mother is an artist. Having graduated from University High School, he attended the University of California, Santa Cruz, before later transferring to the University of California, Los Angeles, from which he graduated in the year 2005 with a Bachelor of Arts in political science.

Career

Klein worked in the year 2003 on a primary campaign of Howard Dean in Vermont and did an internship in the year 2004 for the Washington Monthly in Washington, D.C. In the year 2003, he and Moulitsas were two of the first bloggers to report from a political meeting, that of the California State Democratic Party. In the year 2007, on 10 December, Klein transferred his blog full-time to the American Prospect.

In the year 2011 May, when Bloomberg View began, Klein became a writer there in addition to his role at The Washington Post and MSNBC. Klein declared that he would be leaving The Washington Post in the year 2014 January, with the aim of starting a new media firm with several other veteran journalists.  Klein had initially “proposed the formation of an independent, explanatory journalism website—comprising of more than three dozen staffers” and a yearly budget of more than US$10 million not to leave The Washington Post.

Klein was editor-in-chief at Vox, but currently editor-at-large, and previously wrote for and edited Wonkblog at The Washington Post. He often provides political reporting on MSNBC’s The Last Word with Lawrence O’Donnell The Rachel Maddow Show and Hardball with Chris Matthews. He is a former analyst to Countdown together with Keith Olbermann. In the year 2013, on 14 March, The Week magazine announced that Klein was among the many different individuals being considered to host MSNBC’s yet-unknown 8 p.m. weekday prime-time segment that would replace The Ed Show.

In the year 2015, on October Klein, along with Matt Yglesias and Sarah Kliff, launched The Weeds, a Vox podcast of highly detailed discussions on public policy. Klein as well hosts the podcast The Ezra Klein Show. Klein is Vox’s Netflix series Explained executive producer, which debuted in the year 2018. In the year 2019, in October, Klein, along with some reporters from Vox Media, began the Impeachment, Explained podcast.

Awards & Achievements

In the year 2010, he was named Blogger of the Year by The Week magazine as well as The Sidney Hillman Foundation. In the year 2011, he was named one of the 50 most powerful persons in Washington, D.C., by GQ. His blog was also named one of the 25 best financial blogs by Time magazine in 2011.

In 2013, Klein won the Online News Association Award for Best Online Commentary.  He also won the American Political Science Association’s Carey McWilliams Award for “a major journalistic contribution to our understanding of politics.” He appeared as one of 80 men featured in Esquire’s 80th-anniversary issue and in a feature in T magazine.

It’s good to be the daughter of a billionaire.

Floyd Mayweather’s daughter Iyanna Mayweather pleaded guilty Wednesday to aggravated assault with a deadly weapon for stabbing Lapattra Lashai Jacobs in April 2020. And she’s not expected to spend another day in jail.

Court documents obtained by MTO News reveals that Iyanna, 21, agreed to six years of probation, 40 hours of community service and “no harassing, threatening or abusive contact” with Jacobs.

“We resolved the case between Iyanna and the state,” Iyanna’s attorney, Kent Schaffer, said in a statement to “Entertainment Tonight” Thursday. “The agreement calls for Iyanna to be on deferred adjudication for a period of six years after which she will have no felony conviction and the case will be dismissed. She is eligible to have the deferred adjudication end after two years pending her good behavior.”

Iyanna, who was 19 at the time of the incident, was arrested following an altercation over rapper on/off boyfriend NBA YoungBoy. The boxing legend’s daughter allegedly went to YoungBoy’s Houston home at about 1:30 a.m. and confronted Jacobs, who was with him.

Lapattra has reportedly been paid a hefty sum to support this deal as well.
